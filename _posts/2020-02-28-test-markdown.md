The little time that I get between the never-ending deadlines and projects, I more or less spend it on youtube literally watching anything(trust me anything..) . So last week I came across this video playlist by Shane dawson who in my opinion has given a whole new dimension to YouTube drama. The playlist was about jake paul who according to me is an absolute madman ready to do anything for views. Shane connected jake’s qualities with a sociopath. He used certain traits and qualities to make a really vague definition of a sociopath which according to me would fit a lot of people. This made me think who exactly is a sociopath.

The first time I came across this word was when Benedict Cumberbatch said  "I'm not a psychopath, I'm a highly functioning sociopath" in the famous Sherlock Holmes series.
                                        
 
I had absolutely no regrets setting this as my Instagram bio till now. This blog covers my idea of who actually is a sociopath and how is a sociopath different from a psychopath. Psychopaths or sociopaths both can be cold but devious and charming and  lacking emotions. Having no idea about these terms I used these terms interchangeably. But I realised that I can’t just roam around using these terms for anyone who seems to me as a person who lacks emotions or seems a bit crazy to me. These terms are related to mental health and one should be really careful because mental health, in my opinion, is a highly sensitive topic.
 Initially, I thought that these terms collectively refer to someone who is very manipulative and have absolutely no emotions but even Sherlock Holmes debates the difference. Once I did a little research I figured out that a psychopath is someone who has an inherent brain development disorder. It will be safe to consider psychopathy as a biological condition where the parts of the brain that are responsible for empathy and responding to emotional situations are not fully developed. Sociopaths show the same personality traits like manipulative nature and disregard of others feelings but not because they don't have any sense of morality like Psychopaths. It is possible that they had a different kind of upbringing or that they perceive things in a different manner than usual. It is also possible that they have an entirely different system of belief.
Take the example of a serial killer. A serial killer can be a psychopath or a sociopath. If he is a psychopath he has no sense of what is right or what is wrong, he cannot empathise with others but if he is a sociopath he goes about killing people because he thinks what he is doing is right and he has a completely different sense of right and wrong. But it is not like all sociopaths are mass murderers. You may not believe it but many successful entrepreneurs or world leaders like Mark Zuckerberg , Steve Jobs and many others have sociopathic tendencies.I also believe that it's not black and white in a sense that someone is either a sociopath or someone is not a sociopath.
In my opinion, no one is a true sociopath. People can have sociopathic tendencies. some people might have them more than others.
                                                      
 So according to me, Psychopaths and sociopaths both have their minds wired a bit differently from the rest of us. sociopaths do have a sense of right and wrong but their right might just seem a little wrong to the rest of the world.
